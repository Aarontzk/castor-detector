how it works

Track. Classify. Attribute.

Three passes over your trajectory — all on local models, nothing leaves your machine.

01 / track

Dual-reference drift

Every step is embedded once and measured against two references: the previous step and a stable anchor (step 1 or your source document). Drift from both = a step that left the trajectory, not a legitimate topic shift. An NLI cross-encoder checks whether each step actually follows from the last.

02 / classify

Four cascade types

Rule-based signatures separate retrieval (bad source), inference (logical leap), context_poisoning (bad data mid-chain), and confidence_inflation ("maybe" becomes "certainly" with no new evidence — tracked bilingually, EN+ID). The type tells you which fix applies.

03 / attribute

Honest origin candidates

The first step crossing any threshold becomes the origin candidate — reported with a confidence score and method: "threshold-based", always. Candidate identification, never causal proof. You get a place to look, stated honestly.
